About the Short

Synopsis: Alone in the woods, Robert stumbles into the camp of Jacob — a wounded man haunted by unseen watchers. What begins as a wary encounter spirals into paranoia, buried secrets, and the chilling sense that something — or someone — is hunting them both.

Extended Synopsis: Robert ventures into the woods in search of quiet, but instead finds Jacob — an injured, paranoid man convinced something is stalking his camp. Their tense encounter unravels into mistrust, fragments of memory, and unsettling discoveries beneath the forest floor. Shadows move at the edge of sight, whispers bleed into silence, and both men are forced to confront whether the threat is real, imagined, or far more dangerous than either expected.

About the Feature

The feature explores grief, memory, and survival as Robert is drawn into a predator’s game that blurs fear and reality. The short distills the tone and core dynamics, paving the way for the larger story now in development.

Director

Luke Ollerton is an award-winning screenwriter and director whose work explores psychological horror and thriller. His feature Walker’s Game is the winner of the Forward Film & Television Festival (2025) and Cannes Script Festival (2025), and a finalist at 13Horror.com, Cambridge, and Lit Scares.

The short film Walker’s Game is his directorial debut, conceived as a proof of concept for the feature.
